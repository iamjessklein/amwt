Strengthening collective knowledge

Welcome, we're glad you're here

We're a collective of designers, technologists, publishers and library/archive/museum folk who've worked for decades on sharing and caring for digital knowledge.

We've come together now because the digital commons we've loved and labored for is at a critical inflection point where reckless AI, extractive economics, declining freedoms and climate instability have converged into a polycrisis for open knowledge.

But we believe that this moment of rupture also holds possibility and hope, and that there is a growing hunger worldwide for a re-humaning of the web…

for building connective tissue across the disjointed open knowledge ecosystem
for designing new tools and architectures that center reciprocity, sovereignty, equity, transparency and persistence
for rethinking the economics and governance of the digital commons to increase sustainability and accountability
for writing the web's next chapter

So what are we doing about it? Right now, two things.

01
Tapestries

Crafting a new tool for curation and sensemaking across the commons

We're developing a web application called Tapestries that enables anyone to weave together disparate source materials and media from across the web into multilinear narratives, exhibits and knowledge maps that can be shared, collaborated on, and preserved for the long term. Tapestries is about reclaiming the freedom and joy of an earlier web. They can be woven from personal archives or anything online, and play best with the web's most open and ethical platforms, such as the Internet Archive and Wikimedia.

Newsletter & blog

Thinking out loud and connecting the dots

We run a newsletter/blog documenting the process of developing Tapestries, but also exploring broader perspectives about the future of the commons. Out of these reflections and conversations may emerge the next project, or new patterns of collaboration across existing actors.
